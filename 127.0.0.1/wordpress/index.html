About Pain2D

Pain drawings are used to collect pain data from patients. Predicting a disease based on drawn pain points on pain drawings could become an additional tool in future medicine. The idea that a patient goes to a doctor for pain and a program tells the doctor the directions of further tests or even the real disease seems very futuristic. Our long-term goal is to support the doctor in this task by digitizing and automatically classifying pain drawings. To this end, we have been researching an automated classification based on pain drawings since 2017.

Pain2D is a program that was developed for the automated classification of diseases on the basis of pain drawings in paper form for research purposes. Pain2D is currently not a diagnostic tool, but is aimed at scientists, physicians and anyone interested in the automated analysis of pain drawings.
